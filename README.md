# How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?
I interpret user needs by listening to what the customer wanats the software to do and breakaing those ideas into smaller, manageable requirements. Throughout this course, creating user stories helped me focus on the user's perspective instead of just the technical side of development. User stories made it easier to understand what features were most important and helped guide development, testing, and prioritization of the product backlog. 

# How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
I like to approach development by breaking large projects into smaller tasks and completing them one step at a time. Working through short sprints makes it easier to stay organized, adjust to changing requirements, and fix issues before they become bigger problems. In the future, I hope to continue using Agile practices like sprint planning, daily communication, sprint reviews, retrospectives, and maintaining a prioritized backlog because they help keep projects moving in the right direction while allowing flexibility when requirements change.

# What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
Being a good team member means communicating clearly, completing your work on time, and being willing to help others when needed. It also means accepting feedback and using it to improve instead of taking it personally. Throughout this course, I saw how important collaboration is between the Product Owner, developers, and testers. When everyone communicates openly and works toward the same goal, the team can deliver a better product and respond more effectively to customer feedback.
